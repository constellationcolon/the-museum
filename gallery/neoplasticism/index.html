Broadway Boogie Woogie

Artist   Piet Mondrian
Title   Broadway Boogie Woogie
Date   1942–1943
Medium/Materials   Oil
Current Location   MoMA, NYC, United States

Created by Piet Mondrian just after he moved to New York City, Broadway Boogie Woogie was dominantly influenced by the city grid of Manhattan, as well as the Boogie Woogie music that Mondrian loved.

Here again, we see the modern tendency to experiment in the abstract with space, with forms steadily approaching their Platonic ideals. Though Manhattan is a living, breathing city of grey leviathans, Mondrian here takes the bird's eye's view of the city and renders it in primary colours, as if dissecting and separating out the component colours and vibes of the city and its music. These components are then laid out in an orderly, two dimensional grid. In contrast to the

perspective grids beloved of Renaissance painters that aided in portraying a three dimensional representation of life projected onto two dimensions, Mondrian's grid here portrays life strictly compressed into the two dimensional space of the canvas.

It is no wonder then, that Mondrian's work has been so hugely influential on modern graphic and web design. In arranging the overwhelming messiness of Manhattan into the neat, ordered, and aesthetically appealing space of a canvas, Mondrian paved the way forward for ever more complex forms of information to be presented simply in the overwhelmingly two dimensional spaces of the digital age, on paper and electronic screens.
