The Delivery of the Keys

Artist   Pietro Perugino
Title   Delivery of the Keys, or Christ Giving the Keys to St. Peter
Date   1481–1482
Medium/Materials   Fresco
Current Location   Sistine Chapel, Rome

This masterful piece by Perugino showcases the classic Renaissance vanishing point technique, in which the three dimensional space inhabited by humans is translated faithfully unto two dimensional canvases by way of arranging subjects in a grid that converges in a vanishing point on the horizon. Here, the technique is made all the more blatant by the grid of the public piazza on which the disciples congregate, a testament to the flourishing and convergent scientific and artistic worlds of the Renaissance.

In emerging from the Dark Ages, Renaissance painters such as Perugino sought also to reclaim the bygone glory of the Romans and the Greeks, whilst still keeping true to the Christian faith.

Behind the disciples, two Roman triumphal arches flank the sides of an octagonal building reminiscent of the Dome of the Rock in Jerusalem. The scene is set outdoors, in an open, sunlit public square that is the antithesis to the enclosed, dark spaces of the Gothic cathedral, symbolising a return to the public life of scholarship and artistry that flourished in Ancient Greece and Rome.

In one glance, the priorities of the Renaissance man are clear to see via the places on the canvas that various subjects inhabit: at the centre, stands the divine, brought down from its heavenly place above in the Gothic era, and into the secular, empirical space inhabited by the Renaissance Man.
