The Procuress

Artist   Gerrit Van Honthorst
Title   The Matchmaker, or The Procuress
Date   1625
Medium/Materials   Oil on panel
Current Location   Centraal Museum, Utrecht

An archetypal painting from the Dutch Golden Age, The Procuress shows a matchmaker in the midst of gesturing towards a potential customer; the young man holds a purse in his left hand, gesturing towards the lute that the girl is holding with his other hand; for Dutch speakers, this could be a pun on the Dutch word for lute, luit, that could also be taken to mean vagina.

Despite the controversial subject matter, paintings explicitly depicting prostitution are considered to be common in the era. In a departure from tradition, Dutch golden age painters such as Bruegel tended to depict commoners and peasants in their daily life, rather than just the rich, famous, and/or dead subjects

that rich patrons would pay for artists to depict. Here, a shadowy chamber in a brothel is shown to the viewer, a voyeuristic peek into the private lives of shady characters.

Building on the highly popular Caravaggesque style (a painting style involving the dramatic juxtaposition of light and shadow), Van Honthorst employs here his distinctive device of portraying a silhouetted figure in the foreground. Conveniently, the only illumination in the room comes from a lone candle in the centre of the table, rather than the undefined and unseen light source to be found in other Caravaggesques, confining the characters to a dingy, quotidien space that betrays no delusions of grandeur.
